# HAVEN – Locations Page UX & Content Spec

This document defines the UX, layout, and copy direction for `locations.html`, turning it into a premium “Neighbourhoods / Area Guides” hub consistent with the HAVEN homepage and Properties page. [web:6][web:16][web:24][web:35]

---

## 1. Page Hero & Intro

**Current:**

- Eyebrow: `PRIME LOCATIONS`
- H1: `Explore PrestigiousNeighbourhoods`
- Subheading: `Discover Australia's most sought-after addresses.`

**Issues:**

- Missing space: “PrestigiousNeighbourhoods”.
- Hero is very minimal and doesn’t yet match the editorial, cinematic feel of the brand.

**Proposed structure:**

- Eyebrow: `PRIME LOCATIONS`
- H1: `Explore Prestigious Neighbourhoods`
- Subheading:  
  `Discover Australia’s most sought-after addresses, curated by our local experts.`

**Design notes:**

- Reuse the homepage hero typography (same H1 size, weight, letter-spacing). [web:6][web:16]
- Add generous vertical spacing and a full-width hero image or soft gradient background that hints at a map or skyline, similar to high-end real estate landing pages. [web:16][web:24][web:35]

---

## 2. Locations Overview Section

Transform the page into a grid of key cities/suburbs with mini “location cards”.

**Recommended content groups:**

You can start with 3–6 flagship locations and expand later.

Example locations:

1. **Sydney**  
   - Subline: `Harbourside prestige and world-famous waterfronts.`  
   - Bullet highlights:  
     - `Prime suburbs: Mosman, Vaucluse, Bellevue Hill`  
     - `Property mix: Waterfront estates, family homes, luxury apartments`  

2. **Melbourne**  
   - Subline: `European charm, leafy boulevards, and grand estates.`  
   - Bullet highlights:  
     - `Prime suburbs: Toorak, South Yarra, Brighton`  
     - `Property mix: Heritage mansions, designer townhouses, boutique apartments`  

3. **Brisbane & Gold Coast**  
   - Subline: `Sun-drenched homes and luxury beachfront living.`  
   - Bullet highlights:  
     - `Prime areas: New Farm, Ascot, Surfers Paradise, Broadbeach`  
     - `Property mix: Riverfront homes, sky homes, coastal apartments`  

**Design pattern:**

- Use a 3-column grid on desktop, 2 on tablet, 1 on mobile. [web:6][web:12]
- Each “location card” includes:  
  - Photo (hero image of the area).  
  - Name (e.g. `SYDNEY`).  
  - Short lifestyle tagline.  
  - Key highlights list (2–3 bullets).  
  - CTA: `View Sydney homes` → filters Properties page by that location. [web:16][web:24][web:35]

## 3. Neighbourhood Detail Cards (Optional Second Level)

If you want more depth, add neighbourhoods inside each city.

**Structure for a neighbourhood card:**

- Name: `Mosman, NSW`
- Micro-tagline: `Prestige family living with panoramic harbour views.`
- Short paragraph (2–3 sentences):  
  - Market position (premium, waterfront, family-oriented).  
  - Lifestyle (cafés, schools, parks, marinas). [web:12][web:35]
- Stats line (optional):  
  - `Median house price`, `Typical property types`, `Average days on market` (even if placeholder to start). [web:6][web:12]
- CTA: `View homes in Mosman` (link to filtered Properties).  

Design tips:

- Use a consistent card style as Property cards but with more text and less specs.  
- Keep content scannable, like a magazine-style area guide. [web:16][web:35]

## 4. “Why Buy Here” / Local Expertise Section

Tie locations back to HAVEN’s value proposition and local expertise.

**Content block:**

Heading: `Local insight, global perspective`

Body (2–3 sentences):  
`Our advisors live and work in these neighbourhoods. From school catchments and local cafés to off-market listings, we guide you with insight that goes far beyond the data.` [web:12][web:35]

Design:

- Use a band with a different background (similar to “Why Haven” on home). [web:6][web:16]
- Optionally show 3 mini-pillars:
  - `Neighbourhood advisory`
  - `Off-market access`
  - `Relocation support`

## 5. Call-to-Action: Matchmaking by Location

Add a strong CTA aligned with the “off‑market / advisory” story.

**Suggested section:**

Heading: `Not sure which neighbourhood is right for you?`

Body:  
`Tell us about your lifestyle, family needs, and investment goals, and we’ll recommend neighbourhoods that fit – with specific homes to consider.`

CTA button: `Speak with a local advisor`

Design:

- Use the same CTA style as the appraisal/off-market blocks on other pages. [web:12][web:24]
- Place this midway down the page or right after the main location grid.

---

## 6. SEO & Microcopy Considerations

To align with how top real estate sites treat area pages: [web:16][web:24][web:35]

- Include a short, keyword-rich paragraph near the top:  
  `HAVEN specialises in prestige real estate in Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane, and the Gold Coast – from waterfront mansions and heritage estates to sky homes and boutique apartments.`  
- For each city section, add 2–3 naturally-written sentences mentioning `luxury homes`, `prestige property`, and the city/suburb name.
- Keep copy editorial in tone, not stuffed with keywords.

## 8. Design System Consistency

To keep `locations.html` aligned with `index.html` and `properties.html`: [web:6][web:12][web:16]

- **Typography**
  - Same H1/H2 scale as other pages.
  - Location names use the same style as property card titles or section headers.

- **Color & Imagery**
  - Use the same brand accent colours for highlights and CTAs.
  - All location images should share a similar grade/look (cool/warm, contrast) to feel like one brand set.

- **Layout & Spacing**
  - Section padding and grid gutters match other pages for consistent rhythm.
  - Use the same card corner radius, shadows, and hover behaviour used in properties.
