# HAVEN – About Page UX & Content Spec

This document defines the UX, layout, and copy direction for `about.html`, turning it into a high‑impact brand story and credibility page for HAVEN. [web:2][web:5][web:59][web:61]

---

## 1. Page Hero & Brand Story Intro

**Current:**

- Eyebrow: `OUR STORY`
- H1: `AboutHAVEN`
- Subheading: `Founded in 2001, we've been connecting exceptional people with exceptional properties.`

**Issues:**

- Missing space: `AboutHAVEN`.
- Intro is good but can push more lifestyle and positioning, like strong luxury brokerage sites do. [web:2][web:58][web:61]

**Proposed structure:**

- Eyebrow: `OUR STORY`
- H1: `About HAVEN`
- Subheading:  
  `Founded in 2001, HAVEN is a luxury real estate advisory connecting exceptional people with exceptional properties across Australia’s most coveted postcodes.`

**Design notes:**

- Mirror the hero typography and spacing from the homepage (same H1 scale, alignment). [web:5][web:61]
- Use a full‑width hero image that reflects your brand (elegant interior, architecture, or lifestyle shot) with a subtle overlay so text remains highly legible. [web:2][web:59]

## 2. Brand Philosophy Section (“Where Every Detail Matters”)

**Current:**

- Title: `Where Every DetailMatters`
- Paragraph: `HAVEN was founded on a simple belief: the journey to finding your perfect home should be as exceptional as the home itself.`
- Three pillars:
  - Trusted Advisors – Average 14 years experience in luxury markets.
  - Exclusive Listings – Access off-market properties before public listing.
  - Investment Intelligence – Data-driven insights to maximize your returns.

**Issues:**

- Missing space in heading (`DetailMatters`).
- Pillars are good but could use slightly richer descriptions and consistent formatting.

**Proposed structure:**

Heading: `Where every detail matters`

Intro paragraph (keep and refine):  
`HAVEN was founded on a simple belief: the journey to finding your perfect home should be as exceptional as the home itself – considered, personalised, and completely discreet.`

Three pillars (cards or columns): [web:2][web:5]

- **Trusted Advisors**  
  `An experienced team with an average of 14 years in luxury markets, combining deep local insight with calm, confident negotiation.`

- **Exclusive Listings**  
  `Early access to off‑market and pre‑market opportunities through long‑standing relationships with owners, developers, and family offices.`

- **Investment Intelligence**  
  `Data‑driven advice that considers yield, growth, and risk across key Australian cities – tailored to your portfolio.`

**Design notes:**

- Present the three pillars as cards or columns with subtle icons, matching the style of “Why Haven”/services cards. [web:2][web:5][web:61]
- Ensure consistent spacing, heading levels (H3), and line length for readability.

## 5. “How We Work” / Process Section

Add a section that explains your approach to working with clients, a common pattern on high‑trust real estate sites. [web:2][web:5][web:61]

**Section title:**

`How we work`

Short paragraph:  
`Every client and property is different. We follow a clear, considered process that keeps you informed and in control at every step.`

Simple 3–4 step structure (example):

1. **Understand** – `We begin with a detailed conversation about your goals, timelines, and non‑negotiables.`  
2. **Curate** – `We shortlist opportunities based on insight, not volume – including off‑market and pre‑market options where appropriate.`  
3. **Advise** – `We provide honest recommendations, clear comparables, and negotiation strategies tailored to your situation.`  
4. **Deliver** – `We coordinate every detail – from inspections and marketing to contracts and settlement.`  

Design:

- Use numbered steps with icons or a subtle visual indicator, matching your design system.

## 9. Design System Consistency

To keep `about.html` aligned with your other pages: [web:2][web:5][web:59][web:61]

- **Typography**
  - Same H1/H2 scale as Home and Services.
  - Use consistent heading levels for sections (`Where every detail matters`, `How we work`, etc.).

- **Color & Imagery**
  - Reuse brand accent colour for headings, dividers, and CTA buttons.
  - Maintain consistent photo grading (tone/contrast) with other pages (hero, “Elegant interior” section).

- **Layout & Spacing**
  - Keep section paddings and column gutters identical to other pages (e.g., 80px top/bottom desktop, 48px mobile).
  - Apply the same border radius and shadow to any cards (pillars, values) as used elsewhere.

- **Interactions**
  - Buttons and text links use the same hover and focus states as on Home/Services.
